Viewing your child's prescription through the NHS App

Every parent wants what is best for their child’s health. With prescription view, we want to help you manage your child’s prescriptions with ease.

Prescription view is safe and secure. This new service will allow you to view your child's prescriptions through linked profiles on the NHS App. It has been developed in response to the increasing popularity of online health services.

Benefits of using prescription view

This new service allows you to use the NHS App:

  • view your child’s prescriptions on the NHS App or website
  • use a secure barcode as proof of your child’s prescription at the pharmacy
  • switch between your NHS profile and your child’s linked profile
  • save time when it comes to managing your child’s prescription

In future, the service will allow you to manage your child's healthcare tasks:

  • so you can reduce contact time with your GP surgery
  • at a time that suits you

Prescription view and linked profiles

Before you can sign up to prescription view, you must be:

  • the child’s biological mother
  • able to prove your relationship with the child (for example, with a birth certificate)
  • living with the child at the same address
  • registered at the same GP surgery
  • registered on NHS login and NHS App

Your child must be aged 10 or under before you sign up for prescription view.

If you have more than one child under 10, you can apply for prescription view for them, too.

Getting started with prescription view

Once you are registered on NHS login and the NHS App, you will be asked:

  • about potential safeguarding issues
  • to share your personal information
  • about your relationship to the child
  • about what you want to do on behalf of the child
  • your child’s details

Your child’s data is stored securely in a way that follows the NHS login privacy notice.
